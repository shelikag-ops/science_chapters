Gravity II: Mass vs Weight

Click Next ➡ to begin the adventure!

⚖️

🔭 Today's puzzle

If you weigh 30 kg here, you'd weigh only 5 kg on the Moon. Same body. Different number. WHY?

Imagine going to the Moon. You step on a bathroom scale. The number is now 1/6th of your Earth weight. You haven't lost any of YOU — same arms, legs, brain. So what changed?

And here's the strange flip side: a bag of sugar that says "1 kg" anywhere on Earth — does it also say 1 kg on Mars? Or does it change too?

🤔 Today's mission: learn the most-confused pair of words in science: MASS and WEIGHT. They're NOT the same!
⚖️ ↔ 🌑

⚖️ Concept First: Mass vs Weight Are TWO Different Things

📜 The two definitions:

MASS = the AMOUNT of "stuff" (matter) in an object. Stays the SAME everywhere — Earth, Moon, deep space. Measured in kilograms (kg).

WEIGHT = the FORCE of gravity pulling on that mass. CHANGES based on where you are (which planet, how much gravity). Measured in Newtons (N).

Think of it this way: your MASS is "how much YOU there is." Your WEIGHT is "how hard the planet is pulling on you."

You don't change. The pull does.

🧪 Try It Yourself: Two Scales, Two Truths

Materials: a kitchen balance (the kind with two plates) OR a homemade balance (ruler on a pencil) · a kitchen spring scale (or a fishing scale) · a small object

  1. Balance scale: Put your object on one plate. Add small weights to the OTHER plate until the arms are even. The weights you added = your object's MASS.
  2. Spring scale: Hang the same object from the spring scale. The needle moves because GRAVITY pulls down. The number = WEIGHT.
  3. Notice: balance = comparing two masses → measures MASS. Spring = measures the PULL of gravity → measures WEIGHT.
💡 Big idea: If you took both scales to the Moon:
➡ The balance scale would show the SAME mass (because both sides are pulled equally).
➡ The spring scale would show 1/6 of the weight (because Moon gravity is weaker)!
Same object. Different measurement. That's the difference.

🌍 Evidence: Weight Changes, Mass Doesn't

Take a 30 kg child to different worlds. The MASS stays 30 kg everywhere. WEIGHT changes wildly:

🌍 Earth (g=10)
Weight ≈ 300 N
Mass: 30 kg
🌑 Moon (g≈1.6)
Weight ≈ 50 N (1/6!)
Mass: 30 kg ✓
🔴 Mars (g≈3.7)
Weight ≈ 110 N (~1/3)
Mass: 30 kg ✓
🟠 Jupiter (g≈25)
Weight ≈ 750 N (2.5×)
Mass: 30 kg ✓
🌞 Sun (g≈275)
Weight ≈ 8,200 N (28×)
Mass: 30 kg ✓
🌌 Deep Space (g=0)
Weight = 0 (weightless!)
Mass: 30 kg ✓
🎓 Vocab Snapshot — Who was Newton? The unit of force/weight (the Newton, N) is named after Sir Isaac Newton — the same scientist who discovered gravity (D-1) AND wrote down the 3 laws of motion (C-5, C-6, C-7). When you measure weight in N, you're literally saying "thanks Newton" for figuring all this out!
🔢 The math: Weight = Mass × gravity (in physics: W = m × g). Same mass, different g, different weight!

🚀 MYSTERY #1: Why do astronauts seem "weightless"?

🕵️ Real-life puzzle

Astronauts float around in the International Space Station. Looks like they have no weight! But the ISS is only 400 km up — Earth's gravity there is still about 90% as strong as it is here. So WHY are they floating? (Spiral callback to D-1!)

They're NOT weightless — they're in FREE FALL.

📚 Newton's Cannon analogy (callback to D-1!): Imagine you're on a tall mountain and you fire a cannon SIDEWAYS. Normally the cannonball falls and hits the ground. But if you fire it FAST ENOUGH, it falls so much sideways that the curved Earth keeps dropping away beneath it — and the ball just keeps falling… and falling… and falling. An orbit is just a fall that keeps missing the ground.

The astronauts AND the station are constantly falling toward Earth (gravity's pull is real and strong). But they're also moving sideways VERY fast — so they keep "missing" Earth and curve around it. That's an orbit. Important: gravity is NOT gone — they have weight! They just FEEL weightless because everything around them falls together.

Inside a falling object, anything that's also falling at the same rate FEELS weightless — even though gravity is acting on it. Their mass: same as on Earth. Their weight (force of gravity): nearly same as on Earth. But they FEEL weightless because everything around them is falling together.

"Weightless" is really a feeling, not the science. Their actual weight is close to 90% of their Earth weight. Mind-bending!

⚖️ MYSTERY #2: Why are bathroom scales technically "wrong"?

🕵️ Real-life puzzle

Bathroom scales say something like "30 kg." But scales work using SPRINGS — they measure the force of gravity (= weight in Newtons). So how can they show kg?

Bathroom scales cheat! They actually measure WEIGHT (force in Newtons), then divide by Earth's gravity (g=10) to display "kg." It's a built-in conversion based on Earth's gravity.

If you took your bathroom scale to the Moon, it would show you weighing only 5 kg — but your real mass is still 30 kg. The scale is just doing the wrong math, because it assumes Earth gravity.

Real scientists use BALANCE scales (which compare two masses directly) when they need true mass. Bathroom scales are convenient but technically misleading.

🔄 MYSTERY #3: Could you ever change your MASS?

🕵️ Real-life puzzle

We said mass NEVER changes anywhere in the universe. But some scientists say it CAN change. How?

Mass changes when YOU change physically:

  • 🍎 You eat food → you GAIN mass (the food becomes part of you).
  • 💧 You sweat or use the bathroom → you LOSE mass.
  • 🏋️ You build muscle vs lose fat → you change BODY composition (but total mass changes too).
  • ✂️ You cut your hair → you lose a tiny bit of mass.

So mass DOES change — but only when stuff is added to you or removed from you. Going to the Moon doesn't change your mass — just your weight!

The rule: mass changes when MATTER changes. Weight changes when GRAVITY changes.

⚖️ MYSTERY #4: How does a BALANCE measure mass (not weight)?

🕵️ Real-life puzzle

A balance has two plates. Put your object on one side, add reference weights to the other until they're even. But gravity pulls on BOTH sides equally — so the balance "cancels out" gravity. How does this measure MASS instead of WEIGHT?

Exactly because gravity cancels!

Both sides are pulled DOWN equally hard by gravity. So if you take a balance to the Moon, BOTH sides feel less gravity — and they STILL balance with the same reference weights. The amount of stuff (mass) needed to balance your object is the same anywhere!

This is why scientists use balance scales for true mass: they aren't fooled by changing gravity. Bathroom scales (springs) measure how hard one side is being pulled — that's weight.

Same idea: a see-saw with two equal kids on Earth ALSO balances on the Moon! Gravity pulls both kids equally weaker — they still balance.

📐 Math + Science: The Weight Formula

Here's the simple equation that turns mass into weight:

Weight = Mass × Gravity
(W = m × g)

Where g is the gravitational pull of where you are:

  • 🌍 Earth: g = 10 (in N/kg, simplified for kid-physics)
  • 🌑 Moon: g ≈ 1.6
  • 🔴 Mars: g ≈ 3.7
  • 🪐 Jupiter: g ≈ 25
🧮 Try it: A 5-year-old has mass 20 kg. Calculate their weight on Earth. (Answer: 20 × 10 = 200 N.) On the Moon: 20 × 1.6 = 32 N. Same kid, big weight difference!

⚡ MYSTERY #5: What if Earth's gravity DOUBLED tomorrow?

🌍 Variable Swap thought experiment

Imagine Earth's gravity suddenly became TWICE as strong (g goes from 10 to 20). What happens to:

  • 👤 Your MASS?
  • ⚖️ Your WEIGHT (on a bathroom scale)?
  • 🦴 Your bones (could they handle it)?
  • 🐦 Birds (could they still fly)?

Mass: UNCHANGED (you have the same amount of stuff).

Weight: DOUBLED (gravity pulls you twice as hard). Your bathroom scale would show 60 kg if you used to be 30 kg.

Bones: They'd have to support twice the weight. Many bones would crack/break unless you adapted slowly.

Birds: Wings would have to push 2× harder. Many small birds couldn't generate enough lift; large birds would struggle.

Cars: Engines would have to work harder. Brakes would wear out faster.

Buildings: Most would still stand (they're over-engineered) but bridges could fail!

This is what scientists call "variable swap" thinking — change ONE thing, predict what cascades. SciOly Gold tier!

✨ What you now know

  • MASS = how much "stuff" — never changes (kg)
  • WEIGHT = how hard gravity pulls — changes by location (Newtons)
  • Same body weighs different on Earth, Moon, Mars, Jupiter
  • Astronauts in orbit are NOT weightless — they're in free fall (callback to D-1)
  • Bathroom scales SHOW kg, but really measure weight (and divide by Earth gravity)
  • Balance scales measure TRUE mass (gravity cancels)
  • The formula: Weight = Mass × gravity (W = m × g)
  • Mass only changes when matter changes (eating, sweating)
