CHAPTER 17 · BODY DESIGNS

Body Designs

Some animals are built with bones. Some without. Both work. Let's see how.

🎬 1 video · 🕵️ 3 mysteries · 🦴 Skeleton tour

🔭 Today's question

How does an octopus MOVE without bones?

You move because muscles pull on bones. Octopuses have NO bones. So how do they grab, swim, and squeeze through cracks?

🤔 Today's mission: understand the BIG split in the animal kingdom — those WITH bones inside, those WITHOUT.
🐙

⚔️ The Big Split

🦴 VERTEBRATES

Have a SPINE (backbone) inside.
Examples: humans, dogs, fish, birds, snakes, frogs.
Only ~3% of all animals!

🦗 INVERTEBRATES

NO backbone. Maybe a hard shell outside, or no skeleton at all.
Examples: insects, octopuses, jellyfish, worms, snails.
~97% of animals!

🤯 Surprise: most animals on Earth are invertebrates! We just notice the big vertebrates more.

🦴 Vertebrates — 5 Big Groups

🐟 FISH
Live in water. Gills, scales, fins.
🐸 AMPHIBIANS
Live BOTH in water AND land. Frogs, toads, salamanders.
🐍 REPTILES
Cold-blooded with scales. Snakes, lizards, turtles.
🐦 BIRDS
Feathers, beaks, lay eggs. Most fly.
🐶 MAMMALS
Warm-blooded, hair, milk for babies. (You!)

🦗 Invertebrates — even MORE variety

🦗 Insects
6 legs, 3 body parts (head/thorax/abdomen), often wings. Hard shell outside (exoskeleton).
🕷 Arachnids
Spiders, scorpions. 8 legs, 2 body parts.
🦀 Crustaceans
Crabs, lobsters, shrimp. Hard shells, lots of legs, mostly water-dwellers.
🐙 Mollusks
Snails, octopuses, squid, clams. Soft bodies, sometimes with shells.
🪼 Cnidarians
Jellyfish, corals, anemones. SUPER soft, no skeleton, with stinging tentacles.
🪱 Worms
Long, soft, no skeleton, usually live in soil or water.

🐚 Three Skeleton Strategies

🦴 ENDOSKELETON
Skeleton INSIDE the body. Vertebrates. Strong, can grow with you.
🦀 EXOSKELETON
Hard shell OUTSIDE the body. Insects, crabs. Tough armor, but you have to molt to grow!
🐙 NO SKELETON
Soft body — kept rigid by water pressure inside (hydrostatic). Octopus, jellyfish, worms.
💡 Trade-offs: endoskeleton = grows with you, but bones break. Exoskeleton = tough armor, but you outgrow it. No skeleton = super flexible, but no protection from predators.
🕵️ MYSTERY #1

Why does a crab MOLT?

Every now and then, a crab sheds its entire shell and walks around naked for a few days! Why?

🎯 The shell is solid armor — but that's a problem: it can't grow. So the crab has to MOLT (shed) the old shell, quickly grow a slightly bigger one, and harden up. While the new shell is soft, the crab is super vulnerable. Insects do this too — that's how caterpillars become bigger caterpillars (until the metamorphosis stage). Trade-off of armor: you outgrow it.
🕵️ MYSTERY #2

The Octopus Crack

Octopuses can squeeze through cracks the size of a coin! How?

🎯 No bones! The only hard part of an octopus is its BEAK. Everything else is soft muscle and skin. So they can squish into ANY space their beak fits through. Octopuses use this to escape predators, sneak up on prey, and explore tight spots. Bones make us strong but limit where we can go.
🕵️ MYSTERY #3

Why are insects SMALL?

The biggest insect ever is about the size of your hand. There are no insects bigger than dogs. Why not? Why don't we have horse-sized insects?

🎯 Insects breathe through tiny tubes (no lungs). Big insects can't get enough oxygen to support a big body — the tubes can only deliver oxygen so far. Plus, exoskeletons get HEAVY when they scale up. So insects stay small. (Long ago when air had more oxygen, dragonflies WERE the size of seagulls. But not anymore.) Body design has natural size limits.

✨ What you now know

  • 🦴 Vertebrates have a backbone (~3% of animals)
  • 🦗 Invertebrates don't (~97% of animals)
  • 5 vertebrate groups: fish, amphibians, reptiles, birds, mammals
  • 3 skeleton strategies: endo (inside), exo (outside), none
  • Each strategy has trade-offs (grow vs molt vs flexibility)
  • 🐙 Octopuses squeeze through tiny cracks because no bones
  • 🦗 Insects stay small because of breathing limits
