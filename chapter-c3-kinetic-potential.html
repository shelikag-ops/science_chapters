The Two Faces of Energy

Energy that's MOVING, and energy that's HIDING — ready to pounce.

🎬 1 video · 🎲 Wind-up + dominoes · 🕵️ 2 mysteries

🔭 Today's puzzle

A wound-up rubber band looks the SAME as an unwound one.

Hold a stretched rubber band tight. Now hold a relaxed one. They look identical. But ONE will SNAP and shoot a paper ball across the room. The other won't.

🤔 Today's mission: figure out where energy hides, and how it gets unleashed.
🪀

Two kinds of energy 💡

Scientists split energy into two big buckets:

🏃 KINETIC energy

Energy of motion. Moving things. Anything going, sliding, falling, vibrating, flowing.

From Greek "kineo" = "I move".

💤 POTENTIAL energy

Energy stored up, ready to be unleashed. Stretched, lifted, wound up, charged.

"Potential" = "could happen".

🏦 The Energy Bank

Here's the trickiest thing about POTENTIAL energy — it's already THERE, even when nothing is moving. Think of it like a bank account.

🔋 Potential energy = energy already deposited, waiting to be spent.
A boulder on a cliff has energy in the bank. A stretched spring has energy in the bank. A charged battery has energy in the bank. The object isn't moving — but the energy is REAL and ready.

Common WRONG idea: "The boulder GETS energy when it falls." ❌
Right idea: "The boulder ALREADY HAS energy from being lifted up. Falling just spends it as motion." ✅

🤔 Why does this matter? Because you (or gravity, or the wind) had to do work to put the energy IN the bank. Lifting the boulder up the cliff took energy from your muscles — that energy is now "saved" in the boulder's height. Energy doesn't appear from nowhere when something falls — you put it there earlier!

🎢 The big trick: energy CHANGES form

A roller coaster is the perfect example. Watch the energy flip back and forth:

⛰ At the top
MAX potential energy. Almost no kinetic (barely moving).
⬇️ Going down
Potential SHRINKS, kinetic GROWS. Faster and faster.
🏁 At the bottom
MAX kinetic. Almost zero potential. Going FAST!
⬆️ Climbing next hill
Kinetic shrinks back into potential. Repeat!
🔑 Key idea: energy doesn't disappear — it transforms. Potential ↔ kinetic, like two sides of a coin.
🎲 ACTIVITY · 15 MIN · HANDS-ON

The Domino Energy Storage Test

You'll need: 10–15 dominos (or stiff cards stood on end).

  1. Stand them up in a row, ~1 inch apart.
  2. Just before tipping the first one — STOP. Look at the row. What kind of energy is in those standing dominos? (Hint: stored = potential!)
  3. Now tip the first. What just changed?
  4. Watch the wave. Each domino converts its potential to kinetic, knocks the next, and so on.
👩‍🏫 Teacher note
Teacher: The "lifted" position of each domino is gravitational potential energy. Falling = converts to kinetic. The kinetic energy of falling domino #1 transfers to standing domino #2 → it tips → repeat. Lovely chain of energy transformation.

🧮 Double the Trouble — the math of PE

Potential energy from height has a SECRET formula. Let's discover it without scary symbols.

📐 PE depends on TWO things: how HEAVY × how HIGH.

🔑 Pattern: Double the weight → double the PE. Double the height → double the PE. Double BOTH → quadruple (×4) the PE.

The hidden formula: PE = weight × height (and gravity, but on Earth that's a fixed number ≈ 10).

🕵️ MYSTERY #1 · THINK FIRST

Where does the wind-up toy's energy come from?

You wind up a toy car with a key. Let go. It zooms across the floor. Where did all that movement come from? The car was just sitting there!

🎯 From YOU! When you wound the key, you did work against the spring. Your muscle energy got stored as POTENTIAL energy in the twisted spring. When you release the toy, the spring uncoils, releasing that stored energy as KINETIC energy of the moving car. Energy didn't appear from nowhere — it was YOUR energy, just stored for a moment. Pretty cool magic trick.
🕵️ MYSTERY #2 · THINK FIRST

Why does a higher hill make a faster ride?

Roller coaster designers know: the BIGGER the first hill, the FASTER the ride feels at the bottom. Why is taller hill = faster ride?

🎯 More HEIGHT = more potential energy. When the coaster falls, ALL that potential converts into kinetic energy = speed. A 30m hill stores way more potential than a 10m hill. There's a limit though: real coasters lose some energy to friction (squeaks, sparks, air drag), so the second hill must always be a bit shorter than the first. Energy can never INCREASE on the ride!

  • Energy transforms back and forth — never appears from nothing
  • Examples: roller coaster, wind-up toys, bow & arrow, dominoes

Energy Flow & Loss

Energy can't be made. Can't be destroyed. But it ALWAYS leaks. Why?

🎬 1 video · 🕵️ 4 mysteries

The Conservation Law 📜

Energy can never be created from nothing, and never destroyed. It only changes form or moves around.

This is one of the BIGGEST rules in all of science. We call it the Law of Conservation of Energy. It's true for the entire universe.

So if I push a toy car and it eventually stops… where did the kinetic energy GO? It can't have disappeared!

🔥 The answer: HEAT

When the toy car rolled, it rubbed against the floor (friction). When it slowed down, that "missing" kinetic energy didn't vanish — it turned into heat in the wheels and floor.

🛞 Brake your bike
Brake pads get HOT. That's your kinetic energy → heat.
🤚 Rub your hands fast
They get warm! Muscle energy → friction → heat.
💡 An old light bulb
Most of the electrical energy becomes HEAT (not light). That's why old bulbs are hot.
📝 Big rule: in the real world, every energy transformation loses some energy as heat. Heat slips into the surroundings. We can't get it back.
🕵️ THE ENERGY THIEF · SPIRAL CALLBACK

🏀 Where does the bounce GO?

Drop a basketball from 1 meter. It bounces, but only to about 60 cm. Drop it again — bounces to 35 cm. Again — 20 cm. Soon it's barely bouncing.

🤔 Question: If energy can't be destroyed (Conservation Law), where is it GOING with each smaller bounce?

🎯 The thief is HEAT and SOUND — friction strikes again.

Three places the energy goes on each bounce:
1. Heat in the rubber — the ball squishes on impact and snaps back. That squishing warms it slightly (like bending a paperclip back & forth).
2. Heat in the floor — the floor compresses microscopically and warms too.
3. Sound waves — the "thump" you HEAR is real energy escaping into the air.
4. Air drag — pushing through air on the way down AND up.

🔁 Spiral to C-1: This is the Conservation Law at work. Energy was NEVER lost — it just changed into forms that are hard to track (spread-out heat, scattered sound waves). A "perfect" bouncy ball would have ZERO of these losses — but in the real world, every collision leaks energy.

🌍 Energy FLOW through the world

So if energy keeps leaking as heat, why doesn't everything just stop? Because new energy keeps arriving — from the SUN.

🌞 → 🌳 → 🐰 → 🦊 → 🔥 (heat) → 🌌
The flow: sunlight enters → plants store it as chemical potential energy (Lesson B-3 review!) → animals eat plants and use that energy → energy leaks out as body heat → escapes into space. Every step LOSES some to heat. New sun keeps the system running.
🕵️ MYSTERY #3 · THINK FIRST

Why don't perpetual motion machines work?

Inventors have tried for centuries to build a "perpetual motion machine" — a machine that runs forever without any new energy input. None has ever worked. Why?

🎯 Friction always wins. Every machine has parts that rub. Every rub creates heat. Heat is a one-way street — it leaks into surroundings and you can't catch it back. So machines always lose a bit of energy on each cycle. Eventually, all the kinetic energy is gone (as heat), and the machine stops. The only way to keep going is to add new energy from outside (sun, fuel, batteries). The Law of Conservation of Energy + always-leaking heat = no perpetual motion machine, ever.
🕵️ MYSTERY #4 · THINK FIRST

Why do plants need NEW sunlight every day?

If energy can't be destroyed, why can't plants just save up sunlight from one sunny day and live off it forever?

🎯 Plants DO save some — that's what wood and seeds are. But the plant uses energy every day too: making cells, growing, repairing damage, fighting bugs. Every use leaks some energy as heat into the air. Without new sunlight, the plant slowly bleeds energy and dies. Heat is the universal cost. (Same is true for you — you eat several times a day because your body keeps leaking heat!)
🕵️ MYSTERY #5 · THINK FIRST

The Pendulum Forever?

You pull a pendulum to the side and let go. It swings, swings, swings… and slowly slows down to a stop. WHERE did the energy go?

🎯 Two enemies: air drag (the bob bumps into air molecules) and friction at the pivot (where it's attached). Both turn kinetic energy into heat. After many swings, all the energy has trickled away as warmth in the surrounding air and pivot. If you put the pendulum in a vacuum on a frictionless pivot, it WOULD swing forever — but in the real world, energy always leaks.
🕵️ MYSTERY #6 · THINK FIRST

Why is winter cold even when the sun is still shining?

The sun shines in winter just like in summer. Same sun! Why is it freezing outside?

🎯 In winter, sunlight hits the Earth at a SLANTING angle in your part of the world (because Earth is tilted). The same amount of sunlight gets spread over a bigger area, so each spot gets LESS energy per second. Plus, days are shorter — fewer hours of input. Plus, snow REFLECTS most of the sunlight back instead of absorbing it. Result: less new energy arriving than is leaking away as heat = it gets cold. We'll dig into this in Chapter 39 (D-6 Seasons)!

🧠 Big idea recap

  • ⚡ Two flavors: kinetic (moving) and potential (stored)
  • 🔄 They transform back and forth — never lost
  • 📜 Conservation Law: energy can't be created or destroyed, only moved or changed
  • 🔥 But every transformation leaks heat into surroundings
  • 🌞 That's why life on Earth needs fresh sunlight every day
  • ♾ Perpetual motion is impossible — friction always wins
