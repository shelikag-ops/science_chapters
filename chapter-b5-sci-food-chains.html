Adaptations & Teeth

An animal's body is a story of what it eats. Today we learn to read it.

🎬 1 video · 🕵️ 2 mysteries · 🦷 Tooth detective

🔭 Today's question

A horse and a lion both have teeth. WAY different teeth. Why?

Open a horse's mouth — flat, blocky teeth. Open a lion's mouth — pointy, knife-like teeth. Same animal kingdom, totally different toolkit. What's going on?

🤔 Today's mission: learn to read an animal's body to figure out what it eats.
🐎 ↔️ 🦁

🦷 Two Tooth Toolkits

🐎 Horse teeth (PLANT eater)

  • Flat, wide back teeth
  • Like grinding stones
  • Crush tough grass + leaves
  • No big sharp fangs

🦁 Lion teeth (MEAT eater)

  • Long, pointy fangs
  • Like sharp knives
  • Stab and rip flesh
  • No big grinding back teeth
📜 The Rule: teeth match food. Plants are tough but don't fight back → grinders. Meat is soft but moves → knives.

🍴 The Big Three Eaters

🌿 HERBIVORES
Eat ONLY plants. Cows, horses, deer, rabbits, sheep, elephants.
🥩 CARNIVORES
Eat ONLY meat. Lions, tigers, eagles, sharks, snakes, owls.
🍕 OMNIVORES
Eat BOTH plants AND meat. Bears, pigs, raccoons, humans, chimps.
💡 Quick check: open your own mouth in a mirror. You have flat back teeth (grinders) AND pointier front teeth (cutters). What does that say about humans?

🦴 Body parts tell on you

Teeth are just one clue. Look at the WHOLE animal:

👀 Eyes on the SIDE of the head
Usually a PREY animal (horse, deer, rabbit). Wide vision = spot predators coming.
👀 Eyes on the FRONT
Usually a PREDATOR (lion, eagle, you!). Forward vision = aim at prey, judge distance.
🦵 Long legs
Built for SPEED — to chase or to run away.
🦷 Big claws
Almost always a HUNTER.
🎲 ACTIVITY · 10 MIN

Skull Detective

Look up "horse skull" and "lion skull" on Google Images. Compare:

  1. Where are the eyes? Front or sides?
  2. What do the back teeth look like?
  3. What about the front teeth?
  4. Now do the same for a CAT and a SQUIRREL — predict what they eat from the skull alone.
👩‍🏫 Teacher note
Teacher: Cat = predator → front eyes, fangs. Squirrel = mostly herbivore (with omnivore tendencies) → side-ish eyes, big front incisors for cracking nuts. Real fun to predict before checking!
🕵️ MYSTERY #1 · THINK BEFORE YOU CLICK

The Pleasant Panda Puzzle

Pandas have the digestive system of a CARNIVORE (they're related to bears!). But they only eat bamboo. How does this work?

🎯 Pandas are carnivore-bodied animals stuck eating plants! They eat bamboo for ~14 hours a day because their guts can't extract much energy from it. Many scientists think pandas were originally meat-eaters who switched to bamboo millions of years ago because it was abundant. Their bodies haven't fully caught up. Lesson: sometimes adaptations are mid-evolution and weird.
🕵️ MYSTERY #2 · THINK BEFORE YOU CLICK

The Vampire Vegetarian

You see an animal with sharp pointy teeth. You'd guess: predator! But this animal has ALL pointy teeth — and only eats fruit. Who could it be?

🎯 Could be a fruit bat! They have sharp teeth — used for biting through tough fruit skins. Or some monkeys. Body parts USUALLY match food, but evolution sometimes uses one tool for a different job. Always look at the WHOLE animal, including digestive guts and behavior — not just one body part.

  • 🦷 Teeth match food (grinders for plants, knives for meat)
  • 👀 Eyes on side = prey; eyes on front = predator
  • 🌿 Herbivores · 🥩 Carnivores · 🍕 Omnivores
  • 🐼 Sometimes evolution gives weird mismatches (pandas)

🎯 Listen for: the words PRODUCER (plants) and CONSUMER (animals).

🪪 Who's Who in a Food Chain

🌱 PRODUCERS
Plants. Make their own food from sunlight.
🐰 1° CONSUMERS
Eat plants (herbivores).
🦊 2° CONSUMERS
Eat herbivores (carnivores).
🦅 3° CONSUMERS
Eat other carnivores (top predators).
🍄 DECOMPOSERS
Mushrooms, bacteria, worms — eat dead stuff and return nutrients to soil.
📍 The cycle is the secret. Producers create. Consumers eat. Decomposers recycle. Without ANY of these, the system breaks.

⚡ Energy LOSS at every step

Wild fact: at every level of the chain, ~90% of the energy is LOST as heat. Only 10% goes up to the next level.

🤯 Why this matters: fewer top predators than herbivores. Fewer herbivores than plants. The pyramid HAS to be wide at the bottom, narrow at the top — otherwise there's not enough energy for everyone.

🧮 The Shrinking Energy Pyramid · 10% Rule Math

Time to do the actual math. The "10% Rule" is a SciOly classic.

📐 Rule: Only ~10% of the energy at one level passes UP to the next level. The other 90% is LOST as heat (movement, breathing, body warmth, waste).
LevelWhoEnergy passed upLost as heat
☀️ SunStar10,000 units—
🌱 ProducerGrass1,000 (10% of sun)9,000 → heat
🐰 1° ConsumerRabbit100 (10% of grass)900 → heat
🦊 2° ConsumerFox10 (10% of rabbit)90 → heat
🦅 3° ConsumerEagle1 (10% of fox)9 → heat
🔑 The Pattern: divide by 10 each step UP. Multiply by 10 each step DOWN. To support 1 unit of eagle, you need 1,000 units of grass!

This explains why apex predators (lions, eagles, sharks) are RARE. The pyramid is wide at the bottom because energy gets expensive fast.
🎯 SciOly extrapolation: If a meadow has 50,000 units of grass-energy, how many units make it to a hawk (3° consumer)?
Show answer50,000 → ÷10 = 5,000 (rabbits) → ÷10 = 500 (snakes) → ÷10 = 50 units (hawks). Only 0.1% of the grass energy reaches the hawk!
🕵️ MYSTERY #3 · THINK BEFORE YOU CLICK

The Million-Lion Question

Why doesn't Africa have a million lions? They could just eat more and have more babies, right? Why is the population always limited?

🎯 NOT ENOUGH ENERGY left at their level! Lions are 3rd-level consumers — they only get a fraction of the original sun energy. To feed a million lions, you'd need 10 million zebras to eat, who'd need 100 million worth of grass. Africa's land can only support so many plants → so many zebras → so many lions. The pyramid sets the limit.
🕵️ MYSTERY #4 · THINK BEFORE YOU CLICK

The Vanishing Wolves

People hunted all the wolves out of a forest. Sounds great for the deer, right? But within a few years, the deer were starving and the forest was dying. What went wrong?

🎯 No wolves = no predators on deer = deer population EXPLODES = deer eat ALL the plants and tree shoots = forest can't grow back = then the deer starve. This actually happened in Yellowstone Park in the 1900s. Wolves were reintroduced in 1995 — and the entire ecosystem recovered. Predators ISN'T evil — they keep the system in balance.
🕵️ MYSTERY #5 · THINK BEFORE YOU CLICK

Who Eats Dead Things?

What happens when an animal dies in the forest? Does it just stay there forever? If not, who's eating it?

🎯 DECOMPOSERS! Bacteria, fungi, beetles, worms — all break dead things down. Without them, dead things would pile up forever. Decomposers are the cleanup crew that turns dead matter back into soil nutrients, which feed plants, which feed animals. They're at the BOTTOM of the food chain but they make EVERYTHING work.

♻️ The End is the Beginning · Decomposers as Matter Recyclers

Most kids think decomposers are gross or "the leftovers" of a food chain. WRONG. They're the most important link.

🔁 Spiral to C-4 (Matter vs Energy):
• ENERGY flows ONE WAY through the chain → leaves as heat → escapes to space → never comes back. (That's why we need fresh sunlight every day.)
• MATTER (atoms — carbon, nitrogen, phosphorus) cycles in a LOOP. Every atom in your body was once part of dead plants, animals, and rocks. Decomposers are what make that loop possible.
Without decomposersWith decomposers
🪦 Dead bodies pile up forever (millions of years of leaves, animals, dinosaurs)🪱 Worms, fungi, bacteria break dead matter into soil nutrients
🚫 Atoms locked away → producers run out of nutrients → no new plants🌱 Atoms freed back into soil → producers grow → entire chain restarts
💀 Whole food chain collapses♻️ Endless cycle, sustainable forever
🔑 The Rule of Two Loops:
• Energy: one-way road, sun → producer → consumer → heat → space (gone forever).
• Matter: loop, producer → consumer → decomposer → soil → producer again.
That's why decomposers aren't at the "end" — they close the circle.
🕵️ MYSTERY #6 · THINK BEFORE YOU CLICK

Invasive Disaster

Someone accidentally brought a snake species to a tropical island. Within 30 years, several species of native birds were extinct. How did one snake destroy so much?

🎯 The native birds had NEVER seen this kind of snake before. They had no defenses, no fear, no instinct to fly away. The snake reproduced freely — no predator to control it. Bird population CRASHED. This actually happened on Guam — the brown tree snake wiped out 10 of 12 native bird species. Lesson: introducing a new species into a balanced food chain can be catastrophic. Invasive species are one of the biggest dangers to biodiversity today.

🛣 The One-Way Street · Spiral to B-3 + C-3

Here's the BIG idea every Olympiad student must own: energy is on a one-way street that ends in space.

☀️ → 🌱 → 🐰 → 🦊 → 🔥 → 🌌
🔁 Spiral to B-3 (How plants drink the sun): Plants are the ONLY organisms that capture new energy from outside the planet. Without producers, the food chain has no input.
🔁 Spiral to C-3 (Conservation of Energy): Energy is never destroyed — but every step in the chain spreads it out as heat. Heat is real energy, but it's SCATTERED. We can't catch it back. It eventually radiates into space.
🔑 Why life needs the sun every day:
1. Plants use sunlight to build sugar (energy IN).
2. Animals eat plants (energy moves up the chain, 90% lost as heat at each step).
3. All that heat eventually leaks into space (energy OUT, gone forever).
4. Without TODAY'S sunlight, the system runs down.

Matter recycles. Energy doesn't. If you remember nothing else from B-5, remember THAT sentence.

✨ What you now know

  • 🦷 Body parts tell what an animal eats
  • 🌿 Herbivore · 🥩 Carnivore · 🍕 Omnivore
  • ⛓ Food chain: sun → plant → herbivore → carnivore
  • 📊 90% of energy lost at each step → fewer predators than prey
  • 🍄 Decomposers recycle dead things — ESSENTIAL to the chain
  • ⚖️ Removing or adding any animal can break the whole system
  • 🐺 Predators are GOOD — they keep balance
