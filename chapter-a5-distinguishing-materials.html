🔭 Today's question

How can we TELL materials apart?

You see a clear cup and a clear plastic bag. Both look similar. How do you know which is glass, which is plastic?

🤔 Today's mission: become a material detective — using smart tests!
🔍

🧪 Each Material Has PROPERTIES

👋 How it FEELS
Smooth/rough, soft/hard, hot/cold to touch.
👁 How it LOOKS
Color, shiny/dull, see-through/not.
⚖️ How HEAVY
Same size — feather vs rock are very different!
🔥 How it BREAKS
Brittle (snaps), bendy, stretchy, tough.

Tap each box!

⚖️ The Magic of DENSITY

Density = how much STUFF is packed in a space.

  • A rock and a marshmallow can be the same size — but very different weight.
  • Heavy for its size = HIGH density (rock, metal)
  • Light for its size = LOW density (foam, feather)
💡 You can lift a HUGE pillow easily — but a small rock is HEAVY!

🚿 Float or Sink Test

Floats
Less dense than water — wood, foam, oil, ice
Sinks
More dense than water — rock, metal, glass
🧪 Try it! Drop different things in water — predict first!

🔥 Other Cool Tests

🧲 Magnet test
Iron and steel stick. Plastic, wood, copper don't.
💧 Water test
Some materials soak (paper). Some don't (plastic).
🔥 Heat test
Metal heats fast. Wood is slow. Plastic melts.
🪞 Shine test
Metal is shiny. Cloth is dull.

Tap each box!

🕵️ MYSTERY #1

Why does a giant ship float?

A ship is HEAVY. A pebble is LIGHT. The pebble sinks, the ship floats. How?

It's about DENSITY, not weight! Even though a ship is heavy, most of its inside is AIR. Together, the metal + air is LESS dense than water. So it floats!

🕵️ MYSTERY #2

Why is a feather "light" but a rock "heavy"?

Pick up a basketball-sized feather. Easy! A basketball-sized rock — impossible. Why?

DIFFERENT DENSITIES! Same size, but the rock has WAY more particles packed tight. Different materials have different densities.

🕵️ MYSTERY #3

Why does ICE float on water?

Most things SHRINK when they freeze (denser). Water expands — and floats! Why?

When water freezes, particles arrange in a special pattern with GAPS. Solid ice is LESS dense than liquid water — so ice floats. Lucky for fish in winter!

🔬 Detective Tests You Can Do

Try these on different objects in your house!

  • 🧲 Hold a magnet near it. Sticks? It's probably iron/steel.
  • 💧 Put it in water. Sinks or floats? Tells you density.
  • 🔍 Look at it. Shiny? Smooth? See-through?
  • 👋 Touch it. Cold or warm? Hard or soft?
