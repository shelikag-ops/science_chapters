🔭 Today's question

What is EVERYTHING made of?

Look around. Wood, water, air, your hand. They look different. But — what if they were all made of the SAME tiny things?

🤔 Today's mission: discover the SECRET BUILDING BLOCKS of every single thing.
⚛️

🧱 The BIG Idea: Everything is Made of Tiny Bits

🪵 Wood
Made of TINY particles you can't see.
💧 Water
Made of TINY particles too.
💨 Air
Yep — TINY particles, just farther apart.
👋 You!
You are made of TINY particles!

Tap each box!

🍳 Demo: Sugar in Water

Stir a spoonful of sugar into water. Where did it GO?

  • You can't see it. But it's STILL there!
  • Taste — sweet! The sugar broke into tiny PARTICLES.
  • The particles are now spread between water particles.
💡 Sugar didn't disappear — it just got too SMALL to see!

🔬 The 3 States of Matter

🧊Solid (packed close)
→
💧Liquid (loose)
→
💨Gas (far apart)
💡 Same particles — different SPACING!

🔍 What Makes Each State Different?

🧊 Solid
Particles are STUCK together and packed tight. Can't flow.
💧 Liquid
Particles can SLIDE past each other. Flows but stays together.
💨 Gas
Particles ZOOM around freely. Far apart. Fills any container.

Tap each box!

🌬 Demo: Smell Across the Room

Open a smelly thing (vanilla, perfume, popcorn) on one side. Walk to the other side.

  • You can SMELL it — even far away!
  • Why? Tiny particles ZOOMED through the air to your nose.
  • Gas particles move FAST and SPREAD OUT.
💡 You're literally smelling tiny particles bumping into you!
🕵️ MYSTERY #1

Why does a balloon SHRINK in the freezer?

Blow up a balloon. Put it in the freezer. Take it out 10 minutes later — it's SMALLER. Why?

Cold makes the air particles MOVE SLOWER and squeeze closer together. Less space = smaller balloon!

🕵️ MYSTERY #2

Why can you POUR water but not a brick?

Water flows. A brick doesn't move at all. Same particles, different behavior.

Water particles SLIDE past each other (liquid). Brick particles are STUCK in place (solid). It's all about how the particles can move!

🕵️ MYSTERY #3

Why does ice take up MORE space than water?

Liquid water freezes — and EXPANDS! Cans of soda burst in the freezer. Why?

When water freezes, particles arrange in a special crystal pattern with GAPS. Solid water (ice) is one of the few things that's LIGHTER than its liquid form!

✨ What you now know

  • Everything is made of TINY particles (too small to see)
  • Particles in solids are PACKED, liquids LOOSE, gases FAR APART
  • Heat = particles move FASTER; cold = SLOWER
  • Same matter can change states (ice ↔ water ↔ steam)
  • You're always smelling — and bumped by — tiny particles!
