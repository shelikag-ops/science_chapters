🪨 All About Solids
🪨
A solid keeps its shape all by itself. Leave it alone and it stays the same — it doesn't flow, and it doesn't need a container to hold it together!
💡 Even sugar and sand are solids — they can make a pile that stays put! Liquids always spread out flat.
🪨Rock
✏️Pencil

📚Book

💧 All About Liquids
💧
A liquid flows and pours. It takes the shape of whatever container it's in — but it doesn't fill the whole container. You can always see how full or empty it is!
🌊 Honey flows very slowly, but it's still a liquid! If something flows at all, it's a liquid.
💧Water
🥛Milk
🍯Honey
🧃Juice
🛁Shampoo
🍋Lemonade
💨 All About Gases
💨
A gas spreads out to fill any container completely. You usually can't see gas — but you can feel it! Air is a gas, and it's all around you right now.
👃 Every smell you've ever smelled is a tiny bit of liquid turning into gas and floating to your nose!
💨Air

What is Matter?
Discover the one word that covers every solid, liquid, and gas — and find out what ISN'T matter!
⏱ About 30–40 minutes
🌍 ⚖️ 💡
🌍 What is MATTER?

MATTER = Everything that has WEIGHT and takes up SPACE

🪨 Solid matter
💧 Liquid matter
💨 Gas matter
👤 Your body is matter
🌊 Oceans are matter
🌬️ Air is matter too!
But what about 💡 light and 🔥 heat? They don't have weight and don't take up space — so they're NOT matter. They are forms of ENERGY! A whole different thing.
🧍 Your Body Has All 3 States of Matter!
Can you guess what's inside you before clicking? 👇

🦷 Teeth  |  🦴 Bones  |  💅 Nails  |  💇 Hair  |  🖐️ Skin

The entire planet is made of all three states of matter!
🕵️ Real Life Mysteries — Can You Solve Them?
Think about it first, then click "Reveal Answer"
🚿
Why does your bathroom mirror fog up after a hot shower?
Hot water turns into steam (a gas). When the gas hits the cold mirror, it cools down and turns back into tiny liquid water droplets — that's the fog! Gas → Liquid.
🍦
You hold an ice cream cone on a summer day. Why does it drip?
Your hand and the warm air heat up the solid ice cream. As temperature rises, the solid changes state and becomes liquid. Solid → Liquid (melting)!
🥤
When you open a fizzy drink, bubbles rush out. What are the bubbles?
The bubbles are carbon dioxide — a gas! It was dissolved and trapped in the liquid. When you open the bottle, the pressure drops and the gas escapes as bubbles. It's a gas inside a liquid!
🍪
You're upstairs and smell cookies baking in the kitchen. How?
Every smell is a gas! Tiny gas particles from the warm cookies escape into the air and spread all around — even upstairs. Gas fills every space, remember?
🕯️
A candle has a flame. Is the flame solid, liquid, or gas?
Trick question! A flame is neither. It's light and heat — that's ENERGY, not matter! It has no weight and doesn't take up space. This one fools lots of grown-ups too!
☁️
Clouds float in the sky. Are they solid, liquid, or gas?
Clouds are actually tiny liquid water droplets (or ice crystals — solids!) floating in air. The air (gas) carries them. So clouds can be liquid OR solid — inside a gas!
