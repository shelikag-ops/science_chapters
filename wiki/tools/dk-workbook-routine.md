# DK + Workbook Daily Routine

The 75-min/week bee-prep routine that runs in parallel with Sarah's BFSU chapter work. Designed for May-Oct 2026 (Phase A of the prep plan), reusable through Jan 2027.

**Why this is separate from the chapter app:** Chapters give depth on topics she's already reached in BFSU. This routine gives **breadth** — exposure to bee-syllabus topics she hasn't studied yet, in the recall format bees actually test.

---

## What to buy

**Grade-level reasoning:** Sarah is currently in 2nd grade but will be in **3rd grade for the entire competition window** (Sep 2026 - May 2027). Bee prep books should match her competition tier, not her current grade. Grade 3 material is "stretch but reachable" early on (May-Jun 2026 with parent support) and exactly on-level by the time she starts the qualifier in Sep 2026. By Jan 2027 add Grade 4 stretch material.

**Note:** Spectrum Science Grade 3 series starts at Grade 3 — there is no Grade 2 edition.

## Daily routine (10 min/day, 6 days/week)

| Day | Sarah's 10 min | Parent's job |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | Read 2 facing pages from DK aloud with parent | After: ask 3 quick "what's this?" questions about the diagrams |
| **Tue** | 1 workbook page (~5 questions) from Spectrum Science Grade 3 | Review wrong answers same day; have her redo them |
| **Wed** | Read 2 facing pages from DK | Together: pick 1 word she didn't know → add to **word jar** |
| **Thu** | 1 workbook page from Spectrum Science Grade 3 | Same — review immediately, redo wrong ones |
| **Fri** | Free choice: revisit a DK page she liked, or redo Tuesday's wrong questions | Light touch — let her pick |
| **Sat** (15 min) | Pull 5 random cards from word jar — she defines each. Then 1 question from the [free NSF JSC sample paper](https://www.northsouth.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/JSC_SampleQ.pdf) | The "weekly check-in" — see how her vocab is sticking |
| **Sun** | OFF | OFF |

**Total weekly load:** ~75 min. Add 3 hrs/week of BFSU chapter work and the full "school+" load is ~4.25 hrs/week. Reasonable for a 2nd grader.

## The word jar (the secret sauce)

This is the highest-leverage piece of the routine. Vocabulary recall is what bees test most heavily, and the jar makes vocab building tactile + ownable.

**Setup:**
- Plain 3×5 index cards
- One new word per day from DK reading (Mon, Wed) and any unfamiliar word from workbook (Tue, Thu)
- Sarah owns the jar — she decorates it, names it, etc.

**Card format:**
- **Front:** the word, big, in her handwriting + "What does this mean?"
- **Back:** definition in her own words (parent helps if needed) + a small drawing she does

**Saturday review:**
- Shuffle, pick 5 cards
- She defines each out loud
- Cards she gets right → back of the jar
- Cards she misses → front of the jar (review next week)

**Goal:** ~50 cards by September 2026. By January 2027, she'll have ~100+ words she actually owns.

## Tracking

Print or draw a wall chart on the fridge:

```
Week of ___________

   Mon  Tue  Wed  Thu  Fri  Sat
   [  ] [  ] [  ] [  ] [  ] [  ]
```

She gets a sticker for each completed slot. The chart itself becomes a motivator.

Optional: a separate "word jar count" line where you tick the running card total. Hitting 25 / 50 / 75 / 100 cards earns a small reward.

## Mapping DK sections to BFSU phases

Approximate DK First Encyclopedia of Science sections (varies slightly by edition):

| DK section | Maps to BFSU phase | When to read it |
|---|---|---|
| Living things, Plants, Animals, Human body | Phase 1 (Life) + Phase 3 (Life Depth) | May-Jun 2026 |
| Forces & motion, Energy, Sound, Light | Phase 2 (Energy) + Phase 5 (Forces) | Jun-Aug 2026 |
| Materials, Air, Water, Solids/liquids/gases | Phase 4 (Matter) | Jul-Aug 2026 |
| Earth, Weather, Rocks, Space | Phase 6 (Earth/Space) | Sep-Oct 2026 |

The trick: read DK sections **slightly ahead** of where she is in BFSU. The chapter then becomes the deep dive on something she already has fuzzy familiarity with — easier mastery, deeper retention.

## What to do when she pushes back

She will, eventually. Some honest defaults:

- **"I don't want to do my workbook today."** → Skip it. Two skipped days in a row = drop to 4 days/week for that week. Don't fight.
- **"DK is boring."** → She's burned out on a section. Skip 5 pages forward; let her pick the topic.
- **"My word jar is too full."** → It's not — she's overwhelmed by review. Cut Saturday review to 3 cards, not 5, for 2 weeks.
- **Holiday/travel weeks:** word jar comes along (it's small). Workbook stays home. DK comes along if there's room.

The routine works because it's small and consistent. Don't double up to "make up" missed days — that breaks the small-and-consistent rule.

## Phase B+ (Oct 2026 onward) — what changes

Once chapters are mostly complete (Sep-Oct 2026) and bee attempts begin:

- Drop Spectrum Grade 3 workbook → switch to actual NSB practice questions from the [IAC past questions archive](https://www.iacompetitions.com/national-science-bee-middle-school-and-elementary-past-questions/)
- Keep DK First Encyclopedia reading — now used for *gap-fill* on topics that came up wrong on a bee attempt
- Word jar continues — still the highest-leverage piece
- Add: 1 mock buzzer round per week (parent reads questions aloud, she answers within 5 sec)

## Phase C (Jan 2027 onward) — stretch material

When Spectrum Grade 3 starts feeling easy (typically 4 months into 3rd grade):

- Add **Spectrum Science Grade 4** as the new daily workbook
- Add **DK Science Encyclopedia** (ages 9-12) — replaces or supplements DK First Encyclopedia
- Word jar continues; expect cards to start covering Grade 4-5 vocabulary
- Mock NSB qualifiers move from weekly to twice-weekly
